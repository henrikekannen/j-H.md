## Introducing the Concept
--------------------------
#### What is Holacracy?
<span id="intro2">

- Holacracy = ὁλός, holos (holisitic) and κρατία, kratía (leadership)
- Practice of decentralized management and organizational governance 
- Initially introduced by entrepreneur Brian Robertson in 2007, now trademarked by HolacracyOne, LLC
- Related concepts of flat-organization are are for example Sociocracy or Scaled Agile Frameworks 

</span>

## Roles in Holacracy
---------------------
<span id="roles">

- Member of the of an organization are organized as roles 
- Title of the roles becomes secondary 
- Important: Accountabilities of a role 
- Members may have multiple roles 
- Members design and develop the roles


</span>

---
## New Meeting Format 
---------------------
### Governance Meetings
<span id="gov">

- Held at least once a month 
- Not about operational issues 
- Rather defining, change or abolish roles 
- Discuss internal issues
</span>
---

## New Meeting Format 
---------------------
### Daily Stand up Meetings 
<span id="daily">

- Beginning of a working day (5-10 minutes)
- Quick check-in about the task 
- Every circle member did the previous day and what are their plans for today 

</span>

---

## New Meeting Format 
---------------------
### Tactical Meetings
<span id="tactic">

- Are held once a week 
- Focus on team's operational work 
- Discuss issues that came up lately and to solve problems 
</span>

## Circlestructure of Holacracy
-------------------------------

- Circles are linked together by roles 
- Roles take place in governance meetings and in operational processes 

----
## Circlestructure of Holacracy
- Lead link similar to a manager role
- Lead links assign roles, allocate resources and define the overall strategy 
- Still difference between manager and lead link
- Members of the circle can also influence the role and accountabilities of the lead link

</span>

---
## Integrative decision making 
------------------------------
<span id="decision">

1. Any circle member can propose changes 
2. Members can only propose changes that effect their own work 

</span>

## Hierarchy vs. Holacracy
--------------------------
<span id="table">

| Hierarchy                   | Holacracy                       |
| --------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Top Down Management         | Shared vision Management
| Static job descriptions     | Dynamic roles                   |
| Delegated authority         | Shared authority                | 
| Major reorganizations       | Fast and iterative improvements |
| Firm policies               | Transparent rules               |

</span>
---

## Advantages and Challenges
----------------------------
<h3 style="color:#8dc18d;">ADVANTAGES</h3>

<span id="advantages">

- Increases agility, flexibility, efficiency & transparency
- Encourages team members to take initiative
- Provides a process in which team members ideas and concerns are recognized
- Better use of individual talents
- Reduces the burden of leaders

</span>

---

## Advantages and Challenges
----------------------------
<h3 style="color:red;">CHALLENGES</h3>

<span id="challenges">

- Need for special pre-conditions
- Complex system of rules
- Cultural fit is important
- Self-organization requires a high level of discipline
- Transition from hierarchic management to Holacracy takes time & effort

</span>
